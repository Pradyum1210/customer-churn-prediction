 Business Recommendations
🔹 Introduction

Based on the exploratory data analysis and machine learning model, several key factors influencing customer churn were identified. These insights help in designing targeted strategies to reduce churn and improve customer retention.

🔹 Key Observations
Customers with short tenure (0–12 months) are more likely to churn.
Customers on month-to-month contracts show higher churn rates.
Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to leave.

🔹 Recommendations
1. Improve Early Customer Engagement

New customers are at higher risk of churn, especially within the first year. The company should focus on onboarding programs, regular follow-ups, and personalized support to improve customer experience during the initial stages.

2. Encourage Long-Term Contracts

Customers with long-term contracts are less likely to churn. The business can offer discounts or added benefits to encourage customers to switch from month-to-month plans to yearly or longer-term contracts.

3. Optimize Pricing Strategy

Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn. The company should consider offering flexible pricing plans, bundled services, or discounts to make the pricing more attractive and competitive.

4. Use Predictive Model for Retention

The machine learning model can identify customers who are likely to churn. The company can use this insight to target high-risk customers with personalized retention offers such as discounts, special deals, or improved services.

5. Enhance Customer Experience

Improving service quality, customer support, and overall experience can help in reducing churn. Regular feedback collection and quick issue resolution can increase customer satisfaction.
